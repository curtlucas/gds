Todd Fein

CEO & President

Todd Fein, CEO of Green Diamond, LLC, is a creative and driven executive, with a passion for performance, sustainability, and nearly 20 years of experience in solution-based management and technology consulting. Mr. Fein holds a Systems Science Engineering degree from the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering and Applied Science, with a concentration in environment and materials, and an Economics degree from the Wharton Business School, with a concentration in finance.﻿

Mr. Fein founded Green Diamond to specialize in helping large, complex organizations achieve breakthroughs in next generation solutions for enterprise performance management (EPM), including business intelligence (BI) systems that integrate, analyze, and report on how environmental and social factors account for optimal financial performance. In additon to providing the vision and overseeing key relationships and projects, Mr. Fein also currently serves part-time as the Franklin Fellow for Sustainability Reporting at the US Department of State. While leading a strategic roadmapping and rapid prototyping effort on analytical applications for one of the Department's executive offices﻿ in 2009, Mr. Fein was elected to join the Greening Council Working Group on which Undersecretary of Management and Senior Sustainability Officer Kennedy relies to drive agency sustainability planning, communication and outreach, events, employee ideas, and other functions critical to the success of the Greening Diplomacy Initiative (GDI) that Secretary of State Clinton launched on Earth Day that same year.

Prior to Green Diamond, Mr. Fein founded and served as Director, COO, and CFO of CadenceQuest, Inc, a firm specializing in EPM and BI, again, the latter of which deals with the integration and delivery of the right information to the right people at the right time using powerful analysis and reporting tools. In addition to establishing and overseeing the company’s ongoing management, planning, and budgeting functions, he managed the company’s professional services function and personally oversaw projects for key clients across all sectors, ranging from Wal-Mart, CVS Pharmacy, and Bank of America to the US Dep’t of Health and Human Services, Dep’t of Education, and House of Representatives. In 2008, Mr. Fein initiated a successful exit process for the shareholders.

While founding CadenceQuest, Mr. Fein served at the pleasure of the CTO of the State of New Jersey, the top executive of the State’s Office of Information Technology (OIT), the 1,000+ person, $200+ million central IT organization and former CIO of the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection. There, he supported the CTO on a range of special projects, ranging from framing a leadership program and drafting a white paper on revenue generation to devising strategies for a State-wide IT infrastructure, a virtual online marketplace for economic development, and a State and county customer relationship and service delivery management program.

Prior to that, Mr. Fein helped launch and served as Director of Operations and Corporate Development for Cvent, an online, on-demand event, marketing, and data management and analysis software company that now has 1,100+ employees and 7,500+ customers and runs 750,000+ events and surveys per year for 90,000+ users in 40+ countries. Reporting directly to the CEO, he was responsible for cementing operations through the company’s initial period of growth, including by helping define its mission, values, and vision, its go-to-market strategy, and its product management and other organizational structures.

Prior to Cvent, Mr. Fein was a Manager at American Management Systems (AMS), now CGI, a global management and technology consultancy with more than 25,000 employees and 100 offices. There, he helped build an environmental consulting practice and was part of a team whose work was nominated for archival in the Smithsonian. His hallmark achievement was Tools for Environmental Management and Protection Organizations (TEMPO), an enterprise-wide management system for sustainability that he co-invented, which not only received the first patent in AMS's 30-year history, but which he was also invited to showcase to all governors at their annual association conference. Over $30 million of R&D has been invested in TEMPO, it is listed on the GSA schedule, and many leading public and private sector organizations have adopted it as best practice, from regulatory agencies in states like NJ, MS, LA, KY, MD, NM, and others to industry giants like J&J, DuPont, Merck, and hundreds of others who exchange data with those agencies.
